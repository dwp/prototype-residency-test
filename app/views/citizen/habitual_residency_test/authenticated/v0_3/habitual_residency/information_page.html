You are required to do a Habitual Residency test

To be habitually resident a person must be seen to be making a home in Great Britain. It need not be the only home or a permanent home but it must be a genuine home for the time being.

For example, a short stay visitor or a person receiving short term medical treatment is not resident.

In this test we may ask for evidence regarding:

  • Birth certificates
  • Employment status
  • Passport
  • Other identification documents
  • Other evidence regarding your intent to stay in Great Britain (Doctors notes, Tenancy agreements etc)
